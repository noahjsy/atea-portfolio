UX Designer

Improving digital experiences
through purpose, design, and insight

I specialise in designing and optimising digital platforms — from websites to applications — to make them faster, more intuitive, and easier to use. Grounded in user behaviour and research, I streamline digital workflows and interfaces to reduce friction and improve clarity.

My approach is guided by kaupapa Māori values, bringing depth and intention to digital experiences that serve both people and purpose.

ātea = space

realm of potential and possibility

I see design as a vessel — a way to channel essence, energy, and wairua into the inanimate. Just as every person carries a unique life force, I believe design too can hold and express that same wairua — a presence that goes beyond the visual.

The name Ātea comes from the space that emerged when Rangi and Papa were separated — a sacred opening, full of breath and potential. Ātea represents the moment where life could begin. The breath of life (hau) is what makes us exist, what makes us unique.

My approach to design is grounded in this belief: that essence, meaning, and substance are not just visual — they are spiritual. Through design, I give form to the unseen, and create work that holds space for both people and purpose.
