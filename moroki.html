Moroki Mātauranga Māori Digital Resource Center

A digital resource centre grounded in Te Ao Māori for tamariki and rangatahi to learn mātauranga māori using immersive technology.

My Role

UX Design, UX Research, Copywriting, Brand Identity

This project addresses the lack of modern, accessible resources for Māori—especially tamariki and rangatahi—to engage with Te Ao Māori, mātauranga Māori, and their whakapapa in meaningful ways.

Growing up in Tauranga, I saw how Western media captured young Māori attention. I wanted to create equally engaging tools that inspire pride and curiosity in our own culture to compete with the technological advancements in western media, music and sports.

The idea began with traditional Māori gardening practices and tools like the kō, aiming to reconnect users with the maramataka and mātauranga Māori. But research revealed a strong desire for more interactive, tech-driven experiences—especially among youth. This insight led me to pivot toward a digital solution that balances cultural depth with immersive design.

I started by understanding the current learning needs of tamariki and the gaps in culturally grounded digital resources. My research shifted the focus from a purely physical learning centre to a blended model that integrates technology and Te Ao Māori.

I honoured traditional Māori material resources and taonga in our colour palette—pounamu green, obsidian black, and whalebone white. The bold neon pink evolved from ochre red, representing strength, visibility, and modern expression. This transition symbolises the journey from the natural world to the digital realm.

The app and website was designed as a digital wāhi tapu—a sacred, intentional space. Every element of the interface, from copy to layout, was minimal and meaningful. I used tone and language to cultivate calm, clarity, and cultural respect.
